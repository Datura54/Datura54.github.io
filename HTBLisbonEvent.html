Introduction


The HTB Lisbon meetup was an event organized by Academia de Código. If you were into hacking, cybersecurity, or just curious, this was the place to be. What was it about? It was about why Blue Teams, are the hidden heroes of cyber. These are the experts who defend our digital world from all the bad stuff. Catarina Soeiro, a cybersecurity analyst and part of the Blue Team crew, took the stage to showcase exactly what blue teams do, why they're such a big deal, and why being on the blue side is way cooler than it might sound.

The apresentation covered the following security topics:


• First off, attendees got insights into the tools used by Security Operations Centers (SOCs) for detection. It was a peek into the tech side of things, showcasing how professionals keep an eye on potential threats and what are the best practices to be an efficient SOC team.

• The highlight of the event was a real-world case study. Catarina walked everyone through the whole journey – how they spotted the breach, what steps were taken to address it, and all the actions that followed. It was like stepping into the shoes of investigators as they unraveled a scenario where a hacker successfully infiltrated a system.

• To help break down this complex attack scenario, Catarina introduced a couple of frameworks – the "kill chain" and "MITRE ATT&CK." And some tools that made the detection possible – Splunk (SIEM), Snort (IDS), and Volatility (Forensic Analysis). These tools and frameworks helped organize the attack step by step, giving everyone a hands-on understanding of how these techniques are applied in the real world.

Conclusion


On an ending note, Catarina answered questions from the public, where we talked about more in-depth about some possible usage of other tools and how they would influence our study-case detection capabilities and was at this time that after a question about what she look forward to in the future of SOC, she delved into the possibility of a SOC2.0 where we would not only detect bad actors but also actively look for them.
The event was a unique opportunity to delve into the intriguing world of cybersecurity, learn from an expert like Catarina, and gain valuable insights into blue teams and their pivotal role in safeguarding our digital universe. A big thank you to Academia de Código for organizing this event and to Catarina Soeiro for sharing her expertise and insights with us.
