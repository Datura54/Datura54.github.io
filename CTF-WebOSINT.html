Tasks:

Task 1: Whois Registration
Investigate domain registration details, such as the company name, phone number, nameservers, and registrant's information.

Explore the DNS history of a domain, uncovering its IP addresses over time and identifying the hosting service type.

Examine a domain's historical data, including nameservers, IP addresses, and capture dates by the Internet Archive.

Analyze the internal and external links within a website's article, identify Google Analytics codes, and check for affiliate codes.

Task 6: Final Exam: Connect the Dots
Establish a link between two websites based on DNS records, identifying a common entity.

Objective:

Use WHOIS, DNS history analysis, and web search to uncover security breaches, identify patterns and establish connections for informed conclusions.

Conclusion:

Throughout this investigation, I employed various techniques including WHOIS queries, DNS history analysis, web search skills, and domain correlation to piece together the puzzle of the security breach. By combining these skills, I was able to identify key information, uncover hidden connections, and ultimately make informed conclusions about the nature of the breach and the entities involved. Here's a summary of the tasks and the findings:

Task 1: Whois Registration
Discovered the company name associated with the registered domain: Namecheap Inc. Identified the phone number for the registration company: 6613102107. Found the first nameserver listed for the site: NS1.BRAINYDNS.COM. Obtained the registrant's name, although redacted for privacy. Determined that the registrant's country was Panama.

Task 2: Ghosts of Websites Past
Found the first name of the blog's author: Steve. Uncovered the author's city and country of writing: Gwangju, South Korea. Identified the temple frequently visited by the author: Jeungsimsa Temple.

Task 3: Digging into DNS
Obtained the IP address of the domain "RepublicOfKoffee.com" as of October 2016: 173.248.188.152. Concluded that the target domain likely used shared hosting based on the other domains sharing the same IP. Discovered that the IP address for the domain changed four times over its history.

Task 4: Taking Off The Training Wheels
Found the second nameserver listed for the domain "heat[.]net": NS2.HEAT.NET. Identified the IP address of the domain as of December 2011: 72.52.192.240. Concluded that the domain owner likely used shared hosting based on the domains sharing the same IP. Determined that the site was first captured by the Internet Archive on 06/01/97. Quoted the first sentence of the first body paragraph from the final capture of 2001. Revealed that SegaSoft was responsible for the original version of the site. Displayed the first header on the site in the last capture of 2010: "Heat.net – Heating and Cooling."

Task 5: Taking A Peek Under The Hood Of A Website
Counted the number of internal links within the text of the article: 5. Found the external link in the article: "purchase.org." Identified the Google Analytics code linked to the site: UA-251372-24. Confirmed that the Google Analytics code was not in use on another website. Verified that the link to the website did not have any obvious affiliate codes embedded.

Task 6: Final Exam: Connect the Dots
Confirmed the link between two websites based on DNS records: Liquid Web, L.L.C.
